Laboratory class schedule and list of tasks to be performed

  • LABORATORY 0 : Organizational matters

  • LABORATORY 1 : User interactions using "stdio.h" + simple math calculations

  • LABORATORY 2 : Formatting the printouts + conditional instructions

  • LABORATORY 3 : Simple loop + conditionals

  • LABORATORY 4 : Loops + Iterative algorithms (without arrays yet)

  • LABORATORY 5 : C-arrays

  • LABORATORY 6 : C-arrays + array algorithms (part II)

  • LABORATORY 7 : Introduction to Functions

  • LABORATORY 8 : Functions with parameters + Array Algorithms

  • LABORATORY 9 : C-string, basics of text processing in C

  • LABORATORY 10 : Processing texts in C-string representation (part II)

  • LABORATORY 11 : Using 'struct' type, to represent more complex data

  • LABORATORY 12 : Array of struct (simple database)

  • LABORATORY 13 : Accessing text data files using the <stdio.h> library

  • LABORATORY 14 : Final evaluation
    Students who have already obtained a positive pass, are exempted from participating in these final class.

  

Health and safety rules in the laboratory:

  1. During the first class of the semester the teaching faculty is responsible for training in laboratory health and safety regulations and use of didactic equipment and software
  2. Laboratory users are required to follow health and fire safety regulations.
  3. During didactics students should remain at their work stations and follow activities recommended by the teacher.
  4. All laboratory users should leave their coats, backpacks, sharp and other hazardous objects that could damage laboratory equipment or be dangerous to others in the changing room.
  5. Laboratory users should keep laboratory equipment clean and in good working condition.  
  6. Bringing and consuming food and drinks in the laboratory is prohibited. 
  7. It is strictly forbidden to attempt any repairs of computer hardware and any malfunction of computer hardware or software should be reported to the classroom teacher, who should pass this information to the IT service staff on call or the IT service team.
  8. It is prohibited to unplug any cables or wires from computers and plugs in the laboratory without consent from an IT service team member. In case such consent is obtained from an IT service team member the computer workstation should be returned to its previous state at the end of exercise.
  9. In an emergency it is required to comply with the orders of teaching faculty.
